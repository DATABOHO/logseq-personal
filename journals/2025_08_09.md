- Here’s a structured plan you can try to start shifting things back toward how you felt when you were eating simply and feeling better, but also tailored to your current symptoms.
- ⸻
- 1. Goal for the Next 4 Weeks
  •	Reduce inflammation and digestive irritation
  •	Improve posture and mobility to reduce body pain
  •	Re-establish a steady, nutrient-dense routine without triggering foods
- ⸻
- 2. Morning Routine Blueprint
- On Waking (0–15 min after waking)
  •	Hydration:
  •	1 large glass of warm water with lemon (optional pinch of sea salt if you wake up dehydrated).
  •	Supports bowel movement and digestion activation.
  •	Gentle movement (5–10 min)
  •	Cat–cow stretch (opens spine and massages gut)
  •	Hip openers & gentle spinal twists
  •	Chin tucks and shoulder rolls to counter desk posture
  •	Aim: Reduce morning stiffness and stimulate blood flow to the gut.
- ⸻
- Breakfast Strategy (within 60–90 min of waking)
- Since your stomach feels cramped and digestion is sluggish in the morning, you may want to start light and easy-to-digest:
- Option 1 (low-FODMAP / gentle on digestion):
  •	Steamed zucchini or spinach
  •	2 poached eggs or soft scramble in olive oil
  •	Small serving of cooked oats or quinoa (gluten-free) with blueberries
- Option 2 (if you feel like fasting a bit):
  •	Skip solid food initially, have herbal tea (peppermint, ginger, or fennel)
  •	First meal 2–3 hrs later: lentils + cooked veggies + olive oil
- Avoid raw cruciferous veggies or large salads early in the day — these can be hard to digest first thing.
- ⸻
- Mid-Morning Posture Reset
- If you’re working at a desk:
  •	Set a timer every 45 min:
  •	Stand up, stretch chest & hip flexors, walk for 1–2 min
  •	Keep monitor at eye level and chair supporting lower back
  •	Consider a lumbar cushion or standing desk option.
- ⸻
- 3. Food Guidelines for Next Month
  •	Prioritize: Lentils, eggs, cooked vegetables, fruit, olive oil, avocado, lean proteins, and gluten-free whole grains
  •	Reduce / remove:
  •	Added sugar
  •	Gluten-containing foods (trial period of 4 weeks)
  •	Highly processed snacks
  •	Add: Fermented foods like sauerkraut or kefir a few times a week to support gut microbiome.
- ⸻
- 4. Support Digestion
  •	Chew slowly — aim for 20–30 chews per bite
  •	Don’t overload fluid with meals — sip water, but avoid chugging large amounts while eating
  •	Walk 5–10 min after lunch and dinner to help motility.
- ⸻
- 5. Evening Wind-Down
  •	Light dinner 2–3 hours before bed
  •	Gentle spinal stretches before sleep to prevent waking up tight
  •	No screens in bed — better sleep helps reduce inflammation.
- ⸻
- 6. Monitoring Progress
  •	Keep a daily log of:
  •	Foods eaten
  •	Morning digestion feeling (cramping, bloating, bowel movement timing)
  •	Body pain level (0–10 scale)
  •	This will help you see patterns and confirm if gluten or sugar are major triggers.
